Nexus chess

Hello, my name is Nikita Babich (Kongo Landwalker). Nexus chess is my new chess variant. It differs from classical chess by just one added rule, but has a huge impact on gameplay.

01

The rule

Only pieces connected to their king can move.

Some consequences:

  • King is encouraged to be closer to the center of the board
  • Queen is weaker and not independent
  • Matches are longer, scholar's mate is impossible
  • New concept to think about: Will all your pieces be active, or you will have to leave somebody behind to gain tempo?

02

How does castling work?

Because of the fact, that during castling rook also moves, he has to be "active".

On the first picture castling is available, but not in the position on the second picture.

Honestly, in Nexus chess you would not want to castle that often.

03

What about checks and checkmates?

If the piece cannot move, then it can't move to the square where the king is standing, so there is no check.

On the picture white can play Rxf3 (or Nxf3) to break the connection from the black king to the black queen and save their king from the check, because the black queen becomes inactive.
